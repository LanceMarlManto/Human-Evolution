Key Stages in Human Evolution

The remarkable journey from ancient primates to modern humans

Our Evolutionary Timeline

Human evolution occurred over millions of years through successive species that displayed increasingly modern characteristics. Explore the major milestones that shaped our development from tree-dwelling primates to the only surviving human species on Earth.

7-6 million years ago

Sahelanthropus tchadensis

One of the earliest possible human ancestors, showing both ape-like and human-like features. Its foramen magnum (where the spine connects) suggests it may have walked upright.

  • Small brain (350cc, similar to chimps)
  • Thick brow ridges
  • Possible bipedalism
  • Found in Chad (Central Africa)
4.4 million years ago

Ardipithecus ramidus

Discovered in Ethiopia, "Ardi" shows a mosaic of traits suggesting it climbed trees but also walked upright. Its foot had an opposable big toe for grasping branches.

  • Brain size: 300-350cc
  • Woodland habitat
  • Omnivorous diet
  • Reduced canine teeth
3.9-2.9 million years ago

Australopithecus afarensis

The famous "Lucy" belongs to this species. These hominins were fully bipedal but still retained some adaptations for climbing trees.

  • Brain size: 380-430cc
  • Clear evidence of bipedalism
  • Sexual dimorphism (males larger)
  • Found in East Africa
2.3-1.4 million years ago

Homo habilis

The first member of our genus, known as "handy man" for its association with stone tools. Marked the beginning of increased brain size and tool use.

  • Brain size: 550-687cc
  • First stone tool maker (Oldowan tools)
  • Reduced jaw size
  • More human-like teeth
1.9 million - 110,000 years ago

Homo erectus

The first hominin to migrate out of Africa, spreading as far as Indonesia. Used more sophisticated tools and may have controlled fire.

  • Brain size: 850-1100cc
  • First to use fire (possibly)
  • Acheulean hand axes
  • Longer legs, shorter arms
400,000 - 40,000 years ago

Homo neanderthalensis

Our closest extinct human relative, adapted to cold Ice Age Europe. They buried their dead, made tools, and interbred with modern humans.

  • Brain size: 1200-1750cc (larger than ours)
  • Robust, cold-adapted bodies
  • Mousterian tool culture
  • Symbolic behavior (cave art)
300,000 years ago - present

Homo sapiens

Anatomically modern humans emerged in Africa and spread worldwide. Characterized by complex culture, language, and symbolic thought.

  • Brain size: 1200-1400cc
  • High forehead, small face
  • Complex tools and art
  • Agriculture and civilization
